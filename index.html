Abstract

Planning-oriented self-driving systems perform well in environmental perception and accurate control signal regression. However, they still struggle with handling corner cases and lack interpretability. On the other hand, large language models (LLMs) offer robust generalization capabilities that enhance reasoning and transparency in complex scenarios, but they are limited in generating precise control commands and ensuring efficiency. To address these challenges, this paper presents DME-Seek, a unified framework that combines the strengths of LLM-based and planning-oriented driving systems. The framework consists of two main components: the Decision Maker, which leverages the reasoning abilities of LLM to analyze critical elements in the driving scene and generate reliable high-level driving decisions; and the Executor, which uses accurate environmental perception to translate these decisions into precise control signals. In our implementation, we introduce deep reasoning into the Decision Maker, decomposing complex driving decisions into simpler reasoning steps. Using chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting, the system formulates safe and coherent driving strategies. The planning-oriented Executor then integrates these high-level instructions with environmental perception to ensure accurate control. To further enhance reliability, we introduce a trajectory validation mechanism that Decision Maker continuously verifies the Executor's outputs, ensuring robustness and correctness. To optimize both performance and efficiency, the Decision Maker and Executor are designed to operate asynchronously. This allows the system to approach the efficiency of the high-frequency Executor while maintaining the reliability of the Decision Maker. To train and evaluate DME-Seek effectively, we introduce a new benchmark—the Human Driver Behavior and Decision-Making with Chain-of-Thought (HBD++) Dataset. This dataset captures rich and accurate chains of reasoning behind human driving decisions, providing valuable insights into real-world driving behavior. Experiments on both the nuScenes and HBD++ datasets demonstrate that DME-Seek achieves state-of-the-art performance, significantly improving decision accuracy and driving safety.
